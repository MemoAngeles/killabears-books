## Appearance

Bronte looks like he wants to bite your head off.

Dark brown fur, thick and bristling. Yellow spiral eyes that seem to spin when he's agitated (which is often). Sharp teeth bared in what might be a snarl or might just be his face. He wears a gray ribbed turtleneck sweater that somehow makes him look MORE intimidating, not less.

First impression: this bear will hurt you.

Actual truth: this bear would die for you.

---

## Personality

**First Impression:**
- Grumpy
- Aggressive
- Intimidating
- "Leave me alone" energy

**Actual Truth:**
- Fiercely loyal
- Generous soul
- Protective of friends
- Soft heart behind hard exterior

**The Bronte Paradox:**
Everyone's first meeting with Bronte goes the same way: they assume he's dangerous, unfriendly, best avoided. The spiral eyes don't help. The teeth REALLY don't help.

But the bears who stick around learn the truth: Bronte is one of the most loyal, generous souls in the Killaverse. He just... looks like he isn't.

---

## Background

### Why So Grumpy?

Bronte wasn't always this way. Or maybe he was—who knows? What's certain is that the world hasn't been kind to him, and his face reflects that. The spiral eyes came from somewhere (mutation? accident? he doesn't talk about it). The permanent scowl came from experience.

He learned early that looking scary kept threats away. The problem: it also kept friends away. So he doubled down on the scary and decided friends weren't worth the hassle.

He was wrong.

### Finding His People

Somewhere along the way, Bronte found bears who looked past the exterior. Bears who weren't intimidated by the teeth or unnerved by the spiral eyes. Bears who saw the generous soul hiding behind the grumpy facade.

For them, Bronte would do anything.

## The Loyal Heart

When Bronte decides you're worth his loyalty, that's it. Forever. He will:
- Stand between you and danger without hesitation
- Give you his last resource without being asked
- Remember every kindness you showed him
- Forgive almost anything (eventually)

**The catch:** Earning that loyalty takes time. A lot of time. Bronte doesn't trust easily—he can't afford to, looking the way he does. But once trust is established, it's unbreakable.

---

## Role in the Killaverse

Bronte represents the "don't judge a book by its cover" lesson in living form:
- First impressions are misleading
- Scary doesn't mean evil
- Loyalty is worth more than appearance
- Some of the best hearts come in rough packages

**Story Function:**
Bronte could be the ally nobody expected—the intimidating figure who turns out to be the emotional core of a group. He's the one who looks like he'll betray you but would actually die for you.

## The Spiral Eyes

What's the deal with those eyes? Theories:
- Mutation from early experiments
- Magical accident
- Born that way
- He doesn't talk about it

Whatever the cause, the eyes spin slightly when Bronte is stressed, angry, or protective. It's deeply unsettling. Bronte knows it. He's stopped apologizing for it.
