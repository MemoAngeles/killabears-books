## Appearance

Unc looks like he's been tired since before you were born—and he's still here.

Red-orange fur, weathered but well-maintained. A tan cowboy hat that's probably older than most of the crew. And that coat—pink with fur lining, "Sweet P" spelled out in rhinestones on the back. It shouldn't work. On him, it's iconic.

His expression is permanent exhaustion mixed with "I've seen this before and I'll see it again."

---

## Personality

**Core Traits:**
- Old school in every way
- Speaks in lessons
- Tired but still dangerous
- The conscience of The Syndicate

**The Unc Energy:**
Unc has been doing this longer than anyone. He's seen empires rise and fall. He's seen young hotheads become old bosses or early graves. Nothing surprises him anymore, but everything disappoints him a little.

When Unc speaks, people listen—because his advice has kept more bears alive than anything else.

---

## Background

### The Original

Unc was running operations when $terlin was still learning the business. Some say he TAUGHT $terlin. Others say they came up together. What's certain: when The Syndicate took shape, Unc was there.

He could have been the boss. He didn't want it.

*"Too much work. I'd rather advise than decide."*

### The Bearington Transition

When Bearington took over Ursamarch, a lot of the old guard disappeared—arrested, exiled, or worse. Unc survived because he understood something others didn't: adapt or die.

He helped $terlin negotiate the arrangement that keeps The Syndicate operational. He's not proud of all of it. But they're still here.

---

## Role in The Syndicate

Unc is:
- Elder Advisor (wisdom keeper)
- Historian (remembers deals, grudges, alliances)
- Mediator (settles internal disputes)
- Moral Compass (such as it is)

He doesn't run operations anymore. He just makes sure operations don't run off a cliff.

## The "Sweet P" Story

Nobody asks about the rhinestone jacket anymore. Those who did got a look that said "don't." The leading theories:
- A lost love
- A reminder of something
- He just likes it

Unc never explains. The mystery is part of the legend.
