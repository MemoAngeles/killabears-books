## Appearance

Baron Von Fluffington looks every bit the aristocratic aviator.

Beige fur, immaculately groomed. A military pilot cap with a distinctive red band. Aviator goggles covering his eyes, the mark of a flyer. A black top hat with a red hat band. His bearing is noble, his manner refined, his smile practiced to perfection.

He looks like exactly what Bearington wants by his side: a loyal friend from the old guard, a reminder of better days, a confidant who's been there since the beginning.

He is none of these things.

---

## The Double Life

### What Bearington Sees

Baron Von Fluffington is Lord Bearington's oldest friend. They went to the same schools. They attended the same parties. When Bearington rose to power, Fluffington was there—supporting, advising, celebrating.

Bearington trusts him completely.

Bearington tells him everything.

Bearington has no idea.

### What's Actually True

**Baron Von Fluffington is the most dangerous spy in Ursamarch.**

Every secret Bearington shares reaches the resistance within hours. Every plan, every weakness, every moment of doubt—Fluffington remembers it all. He's been feeding information to Bearington's enemies since day one.

He is Bearington's best friend.

He is Bearington's most hated enemy.

Bearington just doesn't know it yet.

## Background

### The Origin of Hatred

Why does Baron Von Fluffington hate Bearington so completely?

The answer lies in the past—before Bearington's rise, before the mutations, before everything changed. Something happened between them. Something Bearington has either forgotten or never knew mattered.

Fluffington remembers.

Every smile he gives Bearington is a lie. Every laugh is a performance. Every moment of "friendship" is another step toward the day when Bearington finally understands who destroyed him.

### The Long Game

Fluffington has been playing this role for YEARS. He's attended Bearington's parties, laughed at his jokes, nodded along with his megalomaniacal speeches. He's watched innocent bears suffer and said nothing—because the mission is more important than his conscience.

The guilt is enormous. The commitment is unwavering.

*"One day, this will all be worth it. One day, he'll know it was me."*
