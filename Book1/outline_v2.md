# Book 1: TIMEFALL - Revised Outline (v2)

## Format Specifications

| Spec | Target |
|------|--------|
| **Word Count** | 15,000-20,000 words |
| **Pages** | ~200 pages |
| **Chapters** | 10 chapters |
| **Words/Chapter** | 1,500-2,000 |
| **Illustrations** | 80-100 total (~8-10 per chapter) |
| **Target Age** | 8-12 (Middle Grade) |
| **Style** | Diary of a Wimpy Kid meets dark fantasy |

---

## Main Characters

### Murphy "Mops" Grimsley
- Janitor at Bearington Tower, invisible to everyone
- Sarcastic, pessimistic, accidentally heroic
- **Roommate**: Lives with Tachyon (they share a tiny apartment)

### Tachyon
- Sentient alarm clock AI, Mops's roommate and only friend
- Sharp, time-obsessed, secretly ancient
- Provides commentary, tactical advice, and companionship

### Mrs. Fuddlesworth
- Dr. Fomo's cat, mysteriously found her way to the rebel hideout
- The rebels consider her a good omen
- She seems to *know things* she shouldn't

### Dr. Fomo
- Secretly imprisoned (publicly "dead")
- Mops discovers he's alive

### Bearington
- Tyrant ruler, deploys doomsday weapon

### Omni Bear
- Superhero who tries to stop the weapon, causes the TimeFall

### Ursoth the Shadowmaw
- Ancient Fire Spirit, appears at the end when Mops lands in the deep past

---

## Foreshadowed Abilities (Mops's Future Primal Era Skills)

These skills should be shown in the modern era, then pay off in the Primal Era (Book 2+):

| Modern Era Moment | Primal Era Payoff |
|-------------------|-------------------|
| Mops mixes cleaning chemicals expertly | **Chemical Warfare**: Makes poisons, explosives, healing salves |
| Knows every air duct, back corridor, blind spot | **Infiltration**: Can sneak into anywhere via service routes |
| Sets up "Wet Floor" signs strategically | **Trap Master**: Sets up deadly traps with obsessive precision |
| Fixes broken equipment with random junk | **MacGyver Improvisation**: Builds weapons from nothing |
| Survives chemical exposure regularly | **Poison Resistance**: Immune to most toxins |
| Nobody notices him | **Stealth via Mundanity**: "Nobody suspects the janitor" |

## Chapter Breakdown (10 Chapters)

### ACT 1: DISCOVERY (Chapters 1-3)

---

**CHAPTER 1: The Invisible Bear**
*~1,800 words | 8 illustrations*

**Plot Points:**
- Introduce Mops mopping Bearington Tower, completely ignored by everyone
- Tachyon introduced as his roommate/alarm clock companion (they banter)
- Show the oppressive Bearington regime (propaganda, surveillance)
- Establish Mops's daily routine of invisibility
- **FORESHADOW**: Mops expertly mixes cleaning chemicals (comments on what NOT to mix)
- **FORESHADOW**: Mops knows every back corridor, every blind spot
- Tachyon detects "anomaly" on restricted sub-levels

**Illustrations:**
1. Mops mopping while bears stride past ignoring him
2. Tachyon (alarm clock) in their tiny shared apartment, displaying a face
3. Propaganda screens showing Bearington's face everywhere
4. Mops in his supply closet sanctuary (organized by "likelihood to explode")
5. Mops mixing chemicals with precision (labeled bottles)
6. Map of air ducts Mops has memorized
7. Tachyon's screen showing "ANOMALY DETECTED"
8. Mops trudging home through propaganda-lined streets

**Chapter End Hook:**
*Running late tomorrow, Mops takes a shortcut through a corridor he's never used before...*

---

**CHAPTER 2: The Secret in the Sub-Levels**
*~1,800 words | 8 illustrations*

**Plot Points:**
- Mops takes back entrance, wrong turn into restricted corridor
- Hides behind a wall, sees something he shouldn't
- Dr. Fomo—alive, in handcuffs—escorted by Bearington soldiers
- But the news showed Fomo DEAD. Under a sheet. Case closed.
- Mops barely escapes detection
- **FORESHADOW**: Uses janitor knowledge to hide in impossible spot
- Returns to normal life, but can't forget what he saw
- Tachyon notices Mops's stress levels are "catastrophically elevated"

**Illustrations:**
1. Mops turning down an unfamiliar corridor (ominous lighting)
2. "RESTRICTED - AUTHORIZED PERSONNEL ONLY" sign
3. Mops pressed against wall, peeking around corner (terrified face)
4. Dr. Fomo in handcuffs, soldiers on either side (dramatic reveal)
5. Split panel: News broadcast showing "Dr. Fomo Memorial" vs. what Mops just saw
6. Mops hiding in impossibly tight space (janitor skill)
7. Mops mopping the next day, distracted, missing spots
8. Tachyon displaying "STRESS LEVEL: CATASTROPHIC"

**Chapter End Hook:**
*I tried to forget. I really did. But some secrets don't stay buried—they crawl back up and demand to be spray-painted on walls.*

---

**CHAPTER 3: Fomo Lives**
*~1,800 words | 8 illustrations*

**Plot Points:**
- Mops can't keep the secret—starts spray-painting "FOMO LIVES"
- Uses his janitor knowledge to find surveillance blind spots
- His graffiti is TERRIBLE at first ("FOMO LIVSE")
- But it starts spreading—other bears copy it
- The phrase appears everywhere, becomes a movement
- **FORESHADOW**: Mops's paint bonds weird, glows in the dark (portal-energy colored)
- Tachyon: "This is statistically inadvisable. I approve."
- Someone starts following Mops

**Illustrations:**
1. Mops in dark alley with spray paint, looking nervous
2. His first tag: "FOMO LIVSE" (misspelled, dripping)
3. Tags spreading across the city (montage panel)
4. Bears whispering, pointing at the graffiti
5. Mops's paint glowing phosphorescent green at night
6. Security cameras everywhere (Mops carefully avoiding them)
7. A shadow following Mops (we don't see who)
8. Mops cornered in an alley—someone grabs him!

**Chapter End Hook:**
*Cliffhanger: Grabbed from behind, bag over his head, dragged into darkness.*

### ACT 2: THE REBELLION (Chapters 4-6)

---

**CHAPTER 4: The Rebels**
*~1,800 words | 8 illustrations*

**Plot Points:**
- Mops dragged to underground hideout, interrogated
- Rebels demand to know who he is, who sent him
- He explains what he saw—Fomo alive, in handcuffs
- They realize they're on the same side
- Mops meets the rebel group (scrappy, determined, underfunded)
- **MRS. FUDDLESWORTH**: Dr. Fomo's cat is here! Found her way to them
- Rebels: "She just... showed up. We think she's a good omen."
- The cat watches Mops with knowing eyes

**Illustrations:**
1. Bag pulled off Mops's head—bright light, shadowy figures
2. Rebel interrogation room (makeshift, intense)
3. Mops explaining frantically (hands waving)
4. Rebels' expressions changing from suspicious to hopeful
5. The rebel hideout revealed (tunnels, stolen tech, maps)
6. Mrs. Fuddlesworth (fluffy cat) sitting on a command table like she owns it
7. The cat staring at Mops (definitely knows something)
8. Handshake or gesture of acceptance—Mops is in

**Chapter End Hook:**
*"We need someone to get us inside Bearington Tower," the rebel leader said. "Someone invisible. Someone nobody notices." They all looked at me. Oh no.*

---

**CHAPTER 5: Training Montage (Gone Wrong)**
*~1,800 words | 10 illustrations*

**Plot Points:**
- Rebels try to train Mops for the infiltration mission
- He is CATASTROPHICALLY bad at everything
- Combat training: keeps apologizing mid-fight
- Stealth training: trips over everything
- BUT his janitor skills shine through unexpectedly
- **FORESHADOW**: Mops sets up "trap" (wet floor signs) that actually works
- **FORESHADOW**: Mops creates distraction with cleaning chemical "smoke bomb"
- Tachyon is converted from alarm clock to proper KillaBit (disc drone)
- Tachyon can now fly, project holograms, analyze threats
- Mrs. Fuddlesworth watches every training session (judging)

**Illustrations:**
1. Mops attempting combat stance (terrible)
2. Mops on the ground after failed move
3. Stealth training—Mops knocking over cans dramatically
4. Rebels facepalming in unison
5. Mops's "wet floor sign trap" actually catching someone
6. Chemical smoke bomb going off (everyone coughing but impressed)
7. Tachyon's transformation: alarm clock → sleek disc drone
8. New Tachyon hovering, projecting tactical hologram
9. Mrs. Fuddlesworth watching from a high shelf
10. Mops and Tachyon: "Ready as we'll ever be" (both look nervous)

**Chapter End Hook:**
*Tomorrow, we rescue Dr. Fomo. If we survive. If I don't trip over my own feet. If Tachyon doesn't "helpfully" announce our presence. This is fine. Everything is fine.*

---

**CHAPTER 6: The Rescue Mission**
*~2,000 words | 10 illustrations*

**Plot Points:**
- The team infiltrates Bearington Tower
- Everything goes wrong immediately
- But Mops's janitor knowledge saves them repeatedly
- He knows every back corridor, air duct, squeaky door
- **FORESHADOW**: Mops rigs a makeshift trap to delay guards
- They reach the detention level
- Find Dr. Fomo's cell—HE'S REALLY HERE
- Fomo warns them: "Bearington's weapon... it's almost ready. You need to go. NOW."
- Alarms start blaring—they're surrounded

**Illustrations:**
1. Team approaching Bearington Tower at night
2. Mops leading through service corridors (confident for once)
3. Close call—guard patrol, everyone pressed against wall
4. Air duct crawling (Mops leading, others struggling)
5. Mops's trap springing—guards slipping/tangled
6. The detention level (ominous, high-security)
7. Dr. Fomo's cell door—Mops's face when he sees him
8. Dr. Fomo looking weak but determined, warning them
9. ALARM LIGHTS flashing red everywhere
10. Surrounded—soldiers, weapons, no way out

**Chapter End Hook:**
*We came to save Fomo. Now we all needed saving. And somewhere above us, I heard something powering up. Something big.*

### ACT 3: TIMEFALL (Chapters 7-10)

---

**CHAPTER 7: Bearington's Wrath**
*~1,800 words | 8 illustrations*

**Plot Points:**
- Bearington deploys the doomsday weapon
- Mass panic—the weapon is meant to destroy EVERYTHING
- Bearington is willing to sacrifice his own army to kill the rebels
- Rebels and prisoners trying to escape the tower
- Mrs. Fuddlesworth somehow appears, leads them to an exit
- The cat DEFINITELY knows things she shouldn't
- Outside, the sky is turning wrong colors

**Illustrations:**
1. Bearington at controls, expression of pure malice
2. The weapon powering up (massive, ominous machinery)
3. Panic in the streets—bears running, screaming
4. The rebel team escaping through chaos
5. Mrs. Fuddlesworth appearing, meowing urgently
6. Following the cat through impossible routes
7. Emerging outside—the sky is WRONG
8. Colors rippling across the sky like torn fabric

**Chapter End Hook:**
*Then I saw him. A flash of red and white armor, flying toward the weapon. Omni Bear. The superhero. He was going to try to stop it. He was going to—*

---

**CHAPTER 8: The Hero's Sacrifice**
*~1,800 words | 8 illustrations*

**Plot Points:**
- Omni Bear attacks the weapon directly
- The weapon doesn't explode—it TEARS
- Time itself rips open
- Portals everywhere, bears being pulled through
- Reality cracking like broken glass
- Mrs. Fuddlesworth meows once, then vanishes through a portal
- The rebels, Fomo, everyone—scattered into the rifts
- Mops and Tachyon falling, reaching for others who disappear

**Illustrations:**
1. Omni Bear (red and white armor) attacking the weapon—heroic
2. The weapon TEARING instead of exploding
3. Time rips spreading across the sky
4. Bears being pulled through portals (dramatic)
5. Reality shown as cracking glass/torn fabric
6. Mrs. Fuddlesworth calmly walking into a portal
7. Dr. Fomo disappearing through a rift, reaching back
8. Mops and Tachyon falling, falling, reaching—

**Chapter End Hook:**
*Everyone vanished toward the same place. But Tachyon and I kept falling. Deeper. Further. The colors around us weren't just wrong—they were ANCIENT.*

---

**CHAPTER 9: Falling Through Time**
*~1,500 words | 8 illustrations*

**Plot Points:**
- Mops and Tachyon falling through impossible colors
- Glimpses of different eras flashing past
- Tachyon glitching, confused about "when" they are
- "Murphy... this is wrong. We're going too far back..."
- Tachyon's secret knowledge starts surfacing (he shouldn't know this)
- They fall past where the others went
- Past civilization, past history, past EVERYTHING
- Landing hard in a world that looks... primal

**Illustrations:**
1. Falling through swirling temporal colors
2. Glimpses of eras: future cities, medieval, ancient (flash panels)
3. Tachyon's screen glitching, showing error messages
4. Tachyon: "TOO FAR. TOO FAR BACK."
5. The others disappearing toward one time period
6. Mops and Tachyon continuing past, alone
7. Ancient, primal landscape approaching fast
8. IMPACT—landing in prehistoric world

**Chapter End Hook:**
*When I opened my eyes, I was lying in ash. The sky was wrong—dominated by a massive planet. And in the distance, I saw something moving. Something MASSIVE. Something that made the ground shake just by walking.*

---

**CHAPTER 10: When Legends Walked**
*~1,800 words | 10 illustrations*

**Plot Points:**
- Mops wakes in Primal Ursus—prehistoric, dangerous, REAL
- Tachyon is glitching hard, barely functional
- The world is dominated by fire, ash, war-smoke
- Mops sees HIM: Ursoth the Shadowmaw
- Not a statue. Not a myth. A living, breathing SPIRIT
- Ursoth senses Mops, turns, approaches
- "You smell like the future."
- Mops: [internal screaming]
- Tachyon: "Murphy... I remember this. I remember HIM."
- CLIFFHANGER: Ursoth speaks again, and reality shifts around his words

**Illustrations:**
1. Mops lying in ash, waking up (battered, confused)
2. Primal landscape—volcanic, primal, sky dominated by gas giant
3. Tachyon flickering, display showing "TEMPORAL ERROR"
4. Something massive in the distance (silhouette through smoke)
5. The ground SHAKING—Mops stumbling
6. First full reveal: Ursoth the Shadowmaw (EPIC full-page spread)
7. Ursoth's shadowy jaw, ethereal mist, war-smoke darkening sky
8. Ursoth looking directly at Mops
9. Mops's terrified face, reflected in Ursoth's ancient eyes
10. Final image: Ursoth speaking, reality warping around his words

**Final Lines:**
*The being that would become a god looked at me—a janitor from the future—and spoke.*

*"You smell like the future. Like broken time. Like... hope."*

*Tachyon whispered: "Murphy... I think I know why we're here."*

*But before he could explain, Ursoth smiled. And when an ancient spirit of war smiles, it's never good news.*
