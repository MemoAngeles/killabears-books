# Ursus Creatures — Naming Reference

*Last Updated: February 21, 2026*

## Naming Philosophy

Ursus has its own fauna with UNIQUE names — do NOT use real-world dinosaur names (Stegosaurus, Pterodactyl, etc.). Create evocative names based on appearance, behavior, or cultural significance.

---

## Thunderbirds (General Term)

**"Thunderbird"** = The umbrella term for ALL dinosaur-like creatures in Ursus (like how we say "dinosaur").

Individual species have their own names within this category.

## Thunderbird Species (from Cards)

### Amber Sunbather
- **Type:** Large FLYING Thunderbird
- **Traits:** Solar sail that absorbs sunlight, fire attacks
- **Card:** "Its sail drinks the sun all morning. By afternoon, everything it touches ignites."
- **Element:** Fire

### Swiftclaw
- **Type:** Fast pack hunters
- **Traits:** Hunt in pairs, evolve/grow stronger
- **Card:** "They hunt in pairs. They kill in seconds."
- **Variants:** Regular Swiftclaw, Charged Swiftclaw (lightning-powered)

### Ridgeback
- **Type:** Armored plated Thunderbird (ground-dweller)
- **Traits:** Plates/scales, tail spikes, protective, used as caravan guards
- **Variants:** Ridgeback Calf → Ridgeback Caravan Guard → Ridgeback Matriarch
- **Card (Matriarch):** "She has buried three Dreadmaws. Their bones mark her territory."
- **Materials:** **Ridgeback Scale Shield** (armor, defense)
- **Element:** Nature

### Shieldhorn
- **Type:** Horned charger, used as war mounts
- **Traits:** Charge attacks, can carry riders
- **Variants:** Shieldhorn Charger, Shieldhorn War Mount
- **Card:** "Rider and mount move as one terrible purpose."
- **Element:** Lightning

### Stormhorn
- **Type:** Lightning-based Thunderbird with frill
- **Traits:** Charge, pierce, grows stronger from kills
- **Card:** "Lightning dances on his frill. Thunder follows his charge."
- **Element:** Lightning

### Apex Dreadmaw
- **Type:** Apex predator, massive
- **Traits:** Charge + Pierce, destroys targets
- **Card:** "Before the bears, there were kings. This one never stopped."
- **Notes:** The ultimate threat in the wild

## Creature Naming Guidelines

When creating new creature names, consider:

1. **Behavior-based:** What does it do? (Sunbather basks, Thunderbird makes noise)
2. **Appearance-based:** What does it look like? (Spikeback, Longsnout, Shellwalker)
3. **Cultural significance:** What do the bears call it? (might vary by tribe)
4. **Sound/Feel:** Names should feel natural to Ursus, not Earth

### Good Examples:
- Sunbather (behavior + warmth)
- Thunderbird (sound + flight)
- Shadowmaw (appearance + danger — also Ursoth's title!)

### Avoid:
- Direct Earth names (T-Rex, Raptor, Triceratops)
- Too-obvious translations (Three-Horn, Spike-Tail)
- Modern/tech-sounding names
