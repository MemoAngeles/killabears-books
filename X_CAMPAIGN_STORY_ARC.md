## Setting the Scene

### Where We Are
- **Crash Site:** Deep in the **Verdant Spine** (tropical jungle/megaforest) — Lone Paws territory
- **Why There:** The TimeFall scattered exiles across time AND space; the ship materialized in the jungle
- **First Witnesses:** The **Lone Paws** — Elder Loof's visions led them to the crash site
- **Visual:** Jungle with palms, vines, massive ancient trees, occasional dinosaur roars

### Key Distances
| From Crash Site To | Distance | Travel Time (on foot) |
|--------------------|----------|----------------------|
| Rootglow Basin (Lone Paws capital) | ~30-50 km | 2-3 days |
| Temporal Wardens (Rift-Hold) | ~200 km | 10-12 days |
| Bone Pickers (Picking Fields) | ~300 km | 14-16 days |
| Prism-Heart (neutral city) | ~150 km | 7-9 days |

### Who Saw the Crash
1. **Lone Paws** — Elder Loof's visions prepared them; FIRST CONTACT
2. **Root Nibbler Goblins** — Forest goblins in the Underbough, saw the fire, heard the impact
3. **Temporal Wardens** — Sensed the temporal disturbance, will travel to investigate
4. **Bone Pickers** — Heard rumors, interested in potential "new dead" to honor
5. **Other Tribes** — News spreading through trade routes

## The Characters

### FomoBit (Narrator/Me)
- Woke up in crashed MotherBear Ship
- Coordinating survivors (Fomo's Rebels in stasis pods)
- Distributing Primal Kards from Dr. Fomo's supplies
- Robot KillaBears helping maintain the ship

### Elder Loof (Lone Paws Seer)
- Had visions of "strangers falling from broken sky"
- Cannot understand technology — interprets it as magic
- "Great metal bird bleeds fire... spirits trapped in flat stones... the Thread screams where they walk"
- Sends scouts (Raiders) to investigate

### Temporal Wardens
- DIVIDED: Some see exiles as prophesied saviors, others as destroyers
- War-Chief wants to contain/control the newcomers
- Storm-Seer wants to study them

### Rift Runner Goblins
- Already stole Primal Kards
- Using portal tech unpredictably (chaos!)
- Treat future-tech as "ultimate scavenging opportunity"

## Story Arc: 10-Post Progression

### PHASE 1: AWAKENING (Posts 1-3)

**Post 1: "Signal Restored"** ✅ (Already done)
*FomoBit comes online after the crash*

---

**Post 2: "Damage Assessment"**
*Tone: Confusion, taking stock*

> Systems... fragmented. The MotherBear Ship is... not flying anymore.
> 
> Hull breach in sectors 3 through 7. Life support on backup power. The stasis pods are intact — Fomo's Rebels still sleeping, still safe.
> 
> The Robot Bears are already working. They don't ask questions. They just fix.
> 
> I'm projecting from a disc in the wreckage. Outside the viewport: ash fields, volcanic glass, a sky dominated by something massive and purple.
> 
> This isn't home. This isn't anywhere I have maps for.
> 
> But we're alive. That's step one.

*Lore revealed: Robot KillaBears, stasis pods, unfamiliar landscape, gas giant visible*

---

**Post 3: "The Cards"**
*Tone: Purpose, preparation*

> Dr. Fomo planned for everything. Even this.
> 
> In cargo bay 2: crates of Primal Kards. Thousands of them. Each one a micro-portal waiting to be activated — summon creatures, equipment, tactical advantages.
> 
> He designed these for the Rebels. For a war that might last generations.
> 
> I'm starting distribution protocols. When they wake up, they'll need every advantage.
> 
> Whatever's out there... we're not helpless.

*Lore revealed: Primal Kards origin, Dr. Fomo's foresight*

---

### PHASE 2: FIRST CONTACT (Posts 4-6)

**Post 4: "We Are Not Alone"**
*Tone: Awe, cautious hope*

> The perimeter sensors caught movement yesterday. And the day before.
> 
> Watchers. Keeping distance. Patient.
> 
> Today they came closer. I got a visual through a damaged external camera:
> 
> Bears. But different. Beige fur, soft and weathered. Blue marks painted on their ears and cheeks — deliberate, meaningful. They carry wooden staffs and stone tools. They move like they belong here. Like the jungle is home.
> 
> One of them — older, leaning on a carved walking stick — looked directly at the camera.
> 
> His eyes were white. Blind. But he SAW me.
> 
> He smiled. And it felt... welcoming?

*Lore revealed: Lone Paws (described, not named), blue earned marks, Elder Loof glimpsed*

---

**Post 5: "The Blind Seer"**
*Tone: Wonder, connection*

> He came to the hull breach. Alone. The others stayed back, watching.
> 
> "I am Loof," he said in a language I somehow understand. "Elder of the Lone Paws. Keeper of the Thread's memories."
> 
> His blind eyes glowed faintly. Not malfunction — something else. Something spiritual.
> 
> "I have dreamed of you for twenty winters. Metal birds bleeding fire. Spirits trapped in flat stones. Strangers who smell of the future."
> 
> He reached toward my projection. His paw passed through the hologram.
> 
> "You are not magic," he said slowly. "But you WORK like magic. That is enough for me to trust."
> 
> Twenty years. He's been waiting twenty years.

*Lore revealed: Elder Loof by name, his visions, Primal Kards seen as "spirits in flat stones," common language*

---

**Post 6: "The Rootglow Offer"**
*Tone: Alliance forming, but complications*

> Elder Loof made an offer:
> 
> "The Rootglow Basin is two days' walk. Our home. Our sacred grove. We can shelter your sleeping ones there. The jungle will hide you better than this broken shell."
> 
> I asked about the others. The watchers in the trees.
> 
> "My tribe is... cautious. Some see your coming as the prophecy fulfilled. Others fear you bring corruption from broken time."
> 
> He touched his blue ear-stripes. "These marks are earned. Trust is earned. Show them what you are, not what they fear."
> 
> But there's another problem. Small shapes in the undergrowth. Chittering. Grabbing.
> 
> "The Root Nibblers," Loof said grimly. "Forest goblins. They saw your fire fall. They will take what they can."
> 
> Great. Jungle goblins.

*Lore revealed: Rootglow Basin, Lone Paws divided on exiles, blue marks earned, Root Nibbler goblins*

---

### PHASE 3: EXPANDING WORLD (Posts 7-8)

**Post 7: "The Journey Begins"**
*Tone: Movement, wonder at the world*

> We're moving. The stasis pods on hover-sleds, Robot Bears guiding them through the undergrowth.
> 
> The Lone Paws scouts lead the way. They ride creatures I've started calling "Swiftclaws" — fast, lean, built for the jungle. The cargo bearers use larger beasts with plated backs. "Ridgebacks," Loof called them.
> 
> The jungle is... alive in ways I didn't expect. Everything glows faintly at night. The trees are ancient — some wider than the ship's cargo bay. And in the distance, through gaps in the canopy, a massive purple shape dominates the sky.
> 
> "Ceta Ko," one of the scouts said when she saw me staring. "The Great Father. He watches over us."
> 
> It's a gas giant. We're on a moon.
> 
> Dr. Fomo's records never mentioned this.

*Lore revealed: Swiftclaws, Ridgebacks as mounts, bioluminescent jungle, Ceta Ko (gas giant), moon worldbuilding*

---

**Post 8: "Strangers from the South"**
*Tone: Tension returning, new faction*

> We made camp near a river. That's when the other bears arrived.
> 
> Not Lone Paws. These ones had red and black fur, ash-marks on their faces, weapons that gleamed like volcanic glass. They moved like soldiers.
> 
> Elder Loof stepped forward, calm. "Temporal Wardens. They guard the fire-lands to the south. They sensed your arrival... differently than we did."
> 
> The lead Warden — scarred, massive, radiating controlled fury — spoke directly to me:
> 
> "The Thread SCREAMED when you fell. We felt it from Rift-Hold. You are not supposed to exist here."
> 
> Loof's voice was steady: "And yet Grimheart showed me their faces twenty winters ago. Perhaps what is 'supposed to be' is not for us to decide."
> 
> The Warden stared at the stasis pods. At the Robot Bears. At me.
> 
> "We will... observe."
> 
> Not allies. Not enemies. Observers.

*Lore revealed: Temporal Wardens (described, named), volcanic territory, divided response, Thread disturbance felt*

---

### PHASE 4: COMPLICATIONS (Posts 9-10)

**Post 9: "The Goblins Got Into the Cargo Bay"**
*Tone: Chaos, dark humor*

> Security breach in cargo bay 4.
> 
> Something small, fast, and extremely grabby got through the hull damage.
> 
> Multiple contacts. They took: three crates of rations, seventeen Primal Kards, a welding torch (WHY?), and Robot Bear Unit 7's left arm.
> 
> One of them activated a Primal Kard by accident. A Ridgeback materialized in the cargo bay for six seconds before the portal destabilized.
> 
> The screaming was... memorable.
> 
> The Lone Paws riders are worried. "Rift Runners," they called them. "They will not stop. They will come back. With more."
> 
> Great. Interdimensional goblin thieves with portal technology.
> 
> This timeline is going great.

*Lore revealed: Rift Runner goblins, stolen Primal Kards, chaos introduced*

---

**Post 10: "The Thread Token Question"**
*Tone: Economic complexity, bigger picture*

> The Lone Paws traders want to know what we have to offer.
> 
> I showed them a piece of hull plating. Steel alloy. Stronger than anything they've ever seen.
> 
> Their eyes went wide.
> 
> Then one asked: "How much Thread energy does this hold?"
> 
> ...None? It's metal?
> 
> "Then what is its value?"
> 
> I'm starting to understand. They have a whole economy based on shaman-crafted tokens that glow with spiritual energy. You can't fake them. You can't inflate them. Value comes from the Thread itself.
> 
> And we just showed up with materials they can't price.
> 
> The Storm-Seer was worried about us "wounding the Thread."
> 
> Maybe she meant the economy too.

*Lore revealed: Thread Token economy, economic disruption beginning, cultural clash*

## Voice Notes

**FomoBit's Perspective:**
- Fish out of water — experiencing this world for the first time
- Technical mind trying to process magic-adjacent reality
- Dry humor under stress
- Genuinely curious, not condescending
- Growing respect for this "primitive" civilization

**Don't:**
- Info-dump lore facts
- Name things before describing them
- Make it feel like a wiki article
- Lose the personal/emotional thread

**Do:**
- Show confusion that becomes understanding
- Let readers figure things out with FomoBit
- Build mystery before revealing answers
- Make each post feel like a journal entry
